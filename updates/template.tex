\section*{Challenges (3 points)}
% List below at least THREE significant challenges
\begin{enumerate}
	\item One thing that is unfortunate is that though crisp-set QCA is relatively easy to understand from a statistical perspective -- all of the statistics are pretty clearly derived when you see the formulas and make sence with set theory -- when you extend this to fuzzy-set QCA it becomes both less frequently discussed and substantially more complicated (the $2^k$ rows in a crisp-set truth table become just, much larger for fuzzy-set QCA). Though the ideas can be easily communicated through crisp-set language, my understanding of these processes in fsQCA are just unsatisfyingly subpar. I'll have to do more research on this in particular (which is hard when there's not enough resources on it), for myself and for the poster presentation.
	\item As far as I'm aware, for the peacekeeping mission variable I'm adding, it really can only be done manually. It's not so many cases that there's no way I could do it manually, but just enough that it's a little bit of a pain. There are datasets of peacekeeping missions, but getting actual dates/process is a bit harder. Oh well. It's good for learning I suppose.
	\item My current main challenge for next week is determining whether or not the difference in the combinations of factors leading to the outcome in question are actually significantly different -- maybe some analysis of the full dataset including all the combinations of factors will be useful, but I'm still foggy on what statistical processes can evaluate this.
\end{enumerate}
